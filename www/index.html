Reference
Allah Before Everything
Every goal serves your deen

Every habit, ambition, and goal must serve your relationship with Allah. Success without obedience is failure, no matter how it looks from outside.

Salah is the foundation

If prayer collapses, everything eventually collapses behind it. Protect the five prayers before anything else competes for your time.

Taqwa
Establish all obligatory worship

The non-negotiables come first — prayer, fasting, zakat when due. These are not optional extras around your goals; they are the frame everything else hangs on.

Abstain from what is prohibited

Taqwa means consciousness of Allah even when no one is watching — especially then.

Patience, gratitude, repentance

These three cycle endlessly. Patience in hardship, gratitude in ease, repentance when you fall short of either.

Silence Is Strength
Speak less, listen more

The disciplined man's silence carries more weight than most men's speech.

Avoid gossip and unnecessary arguments

Most arguments are not about truth — they are about ego. Walk away from the ones that are.

Character & Family
Obey and respect your parents

This duty does not expire with age or disagreement. Their du'a opens doors effort alone cannot.

Good character is worship

The Prophet ﷺ said the heaviest thing on the scale of deeds is good character.

Morning Routine
Fajr — on time, every time

Bismillah before feet touch the floor. Non-negotiable.

Morning adhkar

Ayat al-Kursi, the three Quls, morning supplications. Your armour for the day.

Evening Closure
Close the loop you opened

The morning sets intention. The evening checks it. A day without closure is a day half-lived.

Three honest questions

Did I complete my intention? What did I avoid? What am I grateful for? Two minutes, every night.

The No Shit Rule
Pause before the urge wins

Ask: what happens after I do this? Play the full movie, not just the pleasure.

Daily Limits
Prayer and Quran — on time, with heart

Not just the motions. Presence is the actual requirement.

No shit

Wasteful content, useless talk, draining people, idle hours — all forbidden.

His Traits
▸ Prays every Fajr — on time, with full presence
▸ Reads Quran daily with understanding
▸ Speaks only when he has something useful to say
▸ His word is his bond — he never breaks a promise
▸ He controls his anger. He responds — he doesn't react.
What He Says To You
He prayed Fajr on time this morning. Did you?
He stayed silent when others talked. People respected him more for it.
He is not waiting for the right moment. He created it already.
