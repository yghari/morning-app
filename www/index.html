Ethics & Character
Silence
Speak only when words add real value. Silence is mastery, not absence.
Humility
Your achievements are from Allah. Your failures are yours. Never let success make you forget this.
Gratitude
Count what you have before what you lack. الحمد لله in every moment.
Discipline
Do what you said you would do, when you said you would, regardless of feeling.
Self-Respect
Iron. Non-negotiable. You do not beg, chase, or compromise principles for approval.
Method
Urge Control
The urge is not you. It is a wave. Observe it. Name it. Wait 90 seconds. Watch it lose power. Do not negotiate.
Identity Voting
Every action is a vote for a type of person. The goal is not to be perfect — it is to have the majority of votes go toward who you are becoming.
Khushu' in Salah
Before takbir: breathe and release. In qiyam: understand the words. In ruku': glorify fully. In sujud: pour your heart.
Self-distancing
When reflecting, use your name instead of "I". "[Name] felt this way." This reduces emotional reactivity and improves honest self-assessment.
Seven Rules
I — Allah first in everything
Before any decision: is this pleasing to Allah?
II — Pray on time, always
The five prayers are the skeleton of the day. Everything else is built around them.
III — Keep promises to yourself
Your self-trust is built or destroyed in the promises no one else can hold you to.
IV — Control desires
Every delayed gratification is a vote for Discipline. Every surrender weakens.
V — Speak only when necessary
Guard your speech like you guard your time.
VI — Improve every day
One domain — body, mind, deen, or provision — every single day.
VII — Never quit after a bad day
After any failure — pray two raka'at, reset, begin again. The return defines you.

What This App Is
"This app can structure your reflection and make consistency easier — but the actual rewiring happens in the 2 minutes you spend being honest with yourself in it, not in the code. The radar chart, the named future self, the urge timer — these are scaffolding to make that honesty easier to sustain for months instead of days. The app is the mirror. You are still the one who has to look into it."
6 Identity Categories
Behavioural Science Basis
Identity-based habits
Every logged action is framed as a "vote" for an identity, not a task completed.
Implementation intentions
"If [X], then I will [Y]" format doubles follow-through vs. vague goals (Gollwitzer, 1999).
Self-distancing
Referring to yourself by name reduces emotional reactivity and improves honest self-assessment.
Spaced resurfacing
Revisiting past reflections at intervals prevents identity drift and reinforces long-term commitments.
